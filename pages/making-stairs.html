Making Stairs

Types of stairs builder

  • linear
  • curved
  • spiral (and TarquinSpiralStairBuilder)

Use semisolids

Create stairs with semisolids. You'll save on a ton of BSP cuts. Though be careful with the Semisolid Region Limit Error (made-up name ) – when making a staircase from semi-solids avoid having visible semisolid faces flush with the face of a subtract box. If the boundry of your stairs and the boundry of a subtract box is on the same grid line, it may cause your stair texture to disapear from certain viewing angles in game. Instead, if you have a staircase 4 steps high, create 3 steps, and texture the wall behind them to look like the last step. Players will step up onto the floor above just as if they were going up stairs.

Stylistically, unless required by your levelmore than 8 or so stairs is a bad idea. It doesn't look good, and when a player runs up them their view goes de-dum-dedum-dedum-dedum.

The method in the upper diagram (to the right)is better than the lower diagram, as the semisolid face marked with a * may vanish when looking at the stairs from the front.

An additional reason not to use the stair tool, and instead use the 2D Shape Editor or a semisolid for your stair case is that each stair has a vertex? at its top and bottom, this causes the game to light the visible side of your stair case individually for each stair.
